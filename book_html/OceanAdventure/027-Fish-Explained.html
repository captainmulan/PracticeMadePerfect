📚 Fish Explained

Learn more about ocean fish!

🫁 Breathing Underwater

Fish breathe using gills, which are special organs that take oxygen from the water. Water flows over the gills, and tiny blood vessels absorb the oxygen.

This is very different from how humans breathe - we need to come to the surface to breathe air, but fish can breathe underwater their whole lives!

💡 Fun Fact

Some fish can survive out of water for a short time because they can breathe through their skin!

🌡️ Cold-Blooded Creatures

Fish are cold-blooded, which means their body temperature changes with the water temperature. This is different from warm-blooded animals like humans and marine mammals.

Being cold-blooded helps fish survive in different water temperatures, but it also means they're less active in cold water!

🎨 Scales and Skin

Most fish have scales covering their body. Scales protect the fish from injury and parasites. They're made of the same material as human fingernails!

Fish also have a slimy coating called mucus that helps them swim faster and protects them from infections.

🐠 Schooling Behavior

Many fish swim together in groups called schools. Schooling helps fish in many ways:

  • Protection - Harder for predators to catch one fish
  • Confusion - The school looks like one big animal
  • Efficiency - Swimming together saves energy
  • Foraging - More eyes to find food

🌊 Migration

Many fish migrate, or travel long distances, for food or to have babies. Some famous migrations include:

  • Salmon - Swim from ocean to rivers to lay eggs
  • Tuna - Travel across oceans following food
  • Eels - Migrate thousands of kilometers to spawn

🔬 Fish Diversity

Fish are incredibly diverse! They range from the tiny Paedocypris (8 mm) to the massive whale shark (12 meters). They live in every ocean habitat from coral reefs to the deep sea!

Scientists are still discovering new fish species every year, especially in deep ocean areas that are hard to explore.
