🌟 What are Fish?

Fish are cold-blooded animals that live in water! They breathe using gills, which take oxygen from the water. Fish come in all shapes, sizes, and colors!

There are over 30,000 different species of fish in the ocean - more than all the other vertebrate animals combined!

🏊 How Fish Swim

Fish swim using their fins and tail. Their tail provides most of the power, while fins help them steer and stay balanced.

  • Dorsal fin - On the back, helps keep fish stable
  • Pectoral fins - On the sides, help with steering
  • Tail fin - Provides power for swimming
  • Pelvic fins - Help with balance and braking

🐠 Types of Ocean Fish

The ocean has many different types of fish:

  • Reef fish - Colorful fish that live in coral reefs
  • Open ocean fish - Fish that swim in deep water
  • Bottom-dwelling fish - Fish that live on the ocean floor
  • Schooling fish - Fish that swim together in groups

🎨 Colorful Reef Fish

Coral reefs are home to the most colorful fish in the ocean!

  • Clownfish - Orange with white stripes, live in anemones
  • Angelfish - Beautiful patterns and long fins
  • Butterflyfish - Bright yellow with black spots
  • Parrotfish - Bright colors, eat coral with beak-like teeth

💡 Fun Fact

Some fish can change color to blend in with their surroundings!

🐡 Strange Ocean Fish

Some ocean fish look very strange!

  • Pufferfish - Can puff up into a ball when scared
  • Seahorse - Has a horse-like head, father carries babies
  • Mola mola - Huge flat fish, looks like a giant head
  • Lionfish - Has venomous spines, beautiful stripes

🛡️ Protecting Fish

Many fish populations are threatened by overfishing and pollution. We can help by:

  • Choosing sustainable seafood
  • Not littering in the ocean
  • Supporting marine protected areas
  • Learning about local fish species
