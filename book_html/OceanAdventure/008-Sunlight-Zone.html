🌟 What is the Sunlight Zone?

The Sunlight Zone (also called the epipelagic zone) is the top layer of the ocean where sunlight reaches all the way to the bottom!

This zone goes from the surface down to about 200 meters deep. It's where most ocean life lives because there's plenty of light for plants to grow.

🌿 Life in the Sunlight Zone

This zone is full of life! You'll find:

  • Phytoplankton - Tiny plants that make most of Earth's oxygen
  • Zooplankton - Tiny animals that eat phytoplankton
  • Fish - Colorful fish like clownfish and angelfish
  • Sea turtles - Ancient reptiles that swim in the ocean
  • Dolphins - Smart and playful marine mammals

🪸 Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are found in the Sunlight Zone! They're like underwater cities made by tiny animals called coral polyps.

Coral reefs are home to more than 25% of all ocean species, even though they cover less than 1% of the ocean floor!

💡 Fun Fact

Coral reefs are called the "rainforests of the sea" because they're so full of life!

📏 How Deep is the Sunlight Zone?

The Sunlight Zone goes from the surface down to about 200 meters (650 feet). That's about as deep as two football fields stacked on top of each other!

🐠 Important for Humans

The Sunlight Zone is very important for humans too! It's where most fishing happens, providing food for billions of people.

It's also where many popular beach activities happen, like swimming, snorkeling, and surfing!
