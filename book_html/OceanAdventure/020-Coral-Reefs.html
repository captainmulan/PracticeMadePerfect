🪸 Coral Reefs

The rainforests of the sea!

🌟 What are Coral Reefs?

Coral reefs are underwater structures made by tiny animals called coral polyps. These polyps build hard skeletons out of calcium carbonate, which slowly builds up over thousands of years!

Coral reefs are called the "rainforests of the sea" because they're so full of life and biodiversity!

🏠 Home to Amazing Creatures

Coral reefs are home to more than 25% of all ocean species, even though they cover less than 1% of the ocean floor!

  • Colorful fish - Clownfish, angelfish, butterflyfish
  • Sea turtles - Green turtles, hawksbill turtles
  • Sharks - Reef sharks, nurse sharks
  • Octopus - Clever and colorful

🤝 Coral and Algae Partners

Coral polyps have a special partnership with tiny algae called zooxanthellae. The algae live inside the coral and make food through photosynthesis.

In return, the coral provides the algae with a safe home. This partnership is why coral reefs need clear, shallow water with lots of sunlight!

💡 Fun Fact

The Great Barrier Reef in Australia is so big it can be seen from space!

📏 How Big are Coral Reefs?

Coral reefs can be huge! The Great Barrier Reef is over 2,300 kilometers (1,400 miles) long - that's longer than the entire coastline of California!

🌍 Why Coral Reefs Matter

Coral reefs are incredibly important for our planet:

  • Protect coastlines - Act as barriers against storms
  • Provide food - Fish for millions of people
  • Support tourism - People love snorkeling and diving
  • Medicine - Scientists find new medicines here

⚠️ Protecting Coral Reefs

Coral reefs face many threats like climate change, pollution, and overfishing. We can help by:

  • Reducing pollution that reaches the ocean
  • Choosing sustainable seafood
  • Not touching coral when snorkeling
  • Supporting reef conservation efforts
