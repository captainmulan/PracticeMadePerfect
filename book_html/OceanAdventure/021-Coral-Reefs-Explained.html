📚 Coral Reefs Explained

Learn more about the rainforests of the sea!

🐚 What are Coral Polyps?

Coral polyps are tiny animals related to jellyfish and sea anemones. They have soft bodies with a ring of tentacles around their mouth.

Each polyp builds a hard skeleton out of calcium carbonate (the same material in seashells). When polyps die, their skeletons remain, and new polyps build on top. Over thousands of years, this builds up into coral reefs!

💡 Fun Fact

A single coral polyp is usually only a few millimeters to a few centimeters across!

🤝 The Coral-Algae Partnership

Coral polyps have an amazing partnership with tiny algae called zooxanthellae. The algae live inside the coral's tissues and make food through photosynthesis.

The algae share this food with the coral, and the coral gives the algae a safe home. This partnership is why coral reefs need clear, shallow water with lots of sunlight!

🌈 Types of Coral

There are many different types of coral:

  • Hard corals - Build the reef structure with their skeletons
  • Soft corals - Don't have hard skeletons, look like plants
  • Brain coral - Looks like a human brain
  • Staghorn coral - Branches like deer antlers
  • Fan coral - Shaped like a fan

🏙️ Reef Structure

Coral reefs have different zones, each with different creatures:

  • Reef flat - Shallow area near shore
  • Reef crest - Highest point, waves break here
  • Fore reef - Slope going down into deeper water
  • Lagoon - Calm water behind the reef

⚠️ Coral Bleaching

When coral gets stressed from high water temperatures or pollution, it expels the algae living inside it. This makes the coral turn white - called coral bleaching.

Bleached coral is very weak and can die. Climate change is causing more frequent and severe bleaching events around the world.

🌍 Famous Coral Reefs

Some of the world's most famous coral reefs:

  • Great Barrier Reef - Australia, largest reef system
  • Belize Barrier Reef - Second largest reef system
  • Red Sea Coral Reef - One of the oldest reefs
  • Florida Reef - Only coral reef in continental US
