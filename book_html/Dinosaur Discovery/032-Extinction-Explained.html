📖 Extinction Explained

Learn about the end of the dinosaurs!

🌟 What Happened?

About 66 million years ago, something terrible happened that changed the Earth forever. A massive asteroid, about 10 kilometers wide, crashed into our planet!

This event caused the extinction of all non-avian dinosaurs and many other species. It was one of the biggest mass extinctions in Earth's history.

💡 Fun Fact

The asteroid hit in what is now Mexico, creating a huge crater called Chicxulub!

☄️ The Asteroid Impact

When the asteroid hit Earth, it caused catastrophic effects:

  • Huge explosion - More powerful than billions of nuclear bombs
  • Tsunamis - Massive waves flooded coastlines
  • Fires - Forests burned across the world
  • Dust cloud - Blocked sunlight for years

🌫️ The Long Winter

After the impact, dust and debris filled the atmosphere and blocked the sun. This caused:

  • No sunlight - Plants couldn't grow
  • Cold temperatures - Earth became much colder
  • Food chain collapse - Herbivores starved without plants
  • Carnivores died - No prey to hunt

🦕 Who Survived?

Most dinosaurs couldn't survive these harsh conditions. But some animals did:

  • Birds - The only dinosaurs that survived
  • Small mammals - Could hide and eat different foods
  • Some reptiles - Like crocodiles and turtles
  • Fish and amphibians - Lived in water

🌍 After the Extinction

After the dinosaurs were gone, mammals began to evolve and grow larger. They filled the ecological niches that dinosaurs had left empty.

Eventually, humans evolved from these mammal ancestors. Without the dinosaur extinction, we might not be here today!

💡 Fun Fact

Birds are the only dinosaurs still alive today - they're living dinosaurs!
