📖 Cretaceous Period Explained

Learn more about the last dinosaur age!

🌟 What Was the Cretaceous Period?

The Cretaceous Period was the last period of the Mesozoic Era! It lasted from about 145 million to 66 million years ago.

This was when the most famous dinosaurs lived, including T-Rex and Triceratops. It ended with a mass extinction that wiped out all non-avian dinosaurs.

💡 Fun Fact

The Cretaceous Period is named after the Latin word for "chalk" because of the chalk deposits from this time!

🌍 The Cretaceous World

During the Cretaceous, continents continued to separate and move toward their modern positions:

  • Continents kept drifting apart
  • Sea levels rose and fell
  • Climate was warm worldwide
  • Flowering plants appeared for the first time

🦕 Famous Cretaceous Dinosaurs

Many of the most famous dinosaurs lived during the Cretaceous:

  • Tyrannosaurus Rex - The king of dinosaurs
  • Triceratops - Three-horned plant-eater
  • Velociraptor - Small, fast hunter
  • Ankylosaurus - Armored tank dinosaur

🌸 Flowering Plants

One of the most important changes during the Cretaceous was the appearance of flowering plants (angiosperms):

  • First flowers appeared
  • Plants evolved to attract pollinators
  • Changed how ecosystems worked
  • Provided new food sources for dinosaurs

🐦 The First Birds

Birds evolved from small meat-eating dinosaurs during the Cretaceous. They were the only dinosaurs to survive the mass extinction at the end of the period!

💡 Fun Fact

Birds are the only dinosaurs still alive today!
