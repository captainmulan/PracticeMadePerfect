📖 Triassic Period Explained

Learn more about the beginning of the dinosaur age!

🌟 What Was the Triassic Period?

The Triassic Period was the first period of the Mesozoic Era! It lasted from about 252 million to 201 million years ago.

This was when the very first dinosaurs appeared on Earth. Before dinosaurs, other reptiles ruled the land, but dinosaurs slowly took over!

💡 Fun Fact

The Triassic Period is named after the Trias rocks in Germany where it was first studied!

🌍 The World of the Triassic

During the Triassic, all the continents were joined together in one giant supercontinent called Pangea!

  • The climate was hot and dry in many places
  • There were vast deserts and some forests
  • Oceans covered much of the planet
  • Life was recovering from a huge extinction

🦕 First Dinosaurs

The first dinosaurs were small and walked on two legs. They were fast runners and probably hunted small animals.

  • Coelophysis - A small, fast meat-eater
  • Eoraptor - One of the earliest known dinosaurs
  • Herrerasaurus - An early meat-eating dinosaur

🌿 Other Triassic Life

Dinosaurs weren't the only animals around during the Triassic:

  • Early mammals - Small, shrew-like animals
  • Flying reptiles - Pterosaurs took to the skies
  • Marine reptiles - Ichthyosaurs swam in the oceans
  • Early crocodiles - Ancient relatives of modern crocs

💥 End of the Triassic

The Triassic Period ended with a mass extinction event. Many species died out, but dinosaurs survived and became even more successful in the next period - the Jurassic!

💡 Fun Fact

The mass extinction at the end of the Triassic paved the way for dinosaurs to become the dominant animals!
