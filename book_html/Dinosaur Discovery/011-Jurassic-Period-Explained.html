📖 Jurassic Period Explained

Learn more about the golden age of dinosaurs!

🌟 What Was the Jurassic Period?

The Jurassic Period was the middle period of the Mesozoic Era! It lasted from about 201 million to 145 million years ago.

This is often called the "Golden Age of Dinosaurs" because dinosaurs became the dominant animals on Earth during this time!

💡 Fun Fact

The Jurassic Period is named after the Jura Mountains in France!

🌍 Breaking Up Pangea

During the Jurassic, the supercontinent Pangea started to break apart! This created new oceans and changed the climate.

  • Continents began to separate
  • New oceans formed between land masses
  • Climate became warmer and wetter
  • Lush forests spread across the land

🦕 Famous Jurassic Dinosaurs

Many famous dinosaurs lived during the Jurassic:

  • Brachiosaurus - Huge plant-eater with a long neck
  • Allosaurus - Large meat-eating dinosaur
  • Stegosaurus - Plant-eater with plates on its back
  • Diplodocus - Very long dinosaur with a whip-like tail

🌿 Jurassic Plants

The warm, wet climate was perfect for plants to grow:

  • Giant ferns - Grew in forests and swamps
  • Conifers - Evergreen trees like pine
  • Cycads - Palm-like plants
  • Ginkgo trees - Still exist today!

🦖 The Rise of Meat-Eaters

Large meat-eating dinosaurs became more common during the Jurassic. Allosaurus was one of the top predators, hunting smaller dinosaurs.

These carnivores had sharp teeth and claws, and were fast runners - perfect for hunting prey!

💡 Fun Fact

The biggest Jurassic dinosaur was probably Brachiosaurus, which could reach 26 meters long!
