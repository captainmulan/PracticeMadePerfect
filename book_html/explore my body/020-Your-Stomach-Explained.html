📖 Your Stomach Explained

Learn more about your amazing stomach!

🍽️ What Is Your Stomach?

Your stomach is a J-shaped organ in your belly that helps digest food! It's like a muscular bag that mixes and breaks down the food you eat.

Your stomach is about the size of your fist when empty, but it can stretch to hold about 1 liter of food!

💡 Fun Fact

Your stomach makes a new protective lining every few days to keep the acid from hurting it!

🔬 How Digestion Works

When food enters your stomach, special muscles squeeze and mix it like a blender. Your stomach also makes strong acid that helps break down food into smaller pieces.

The acid in your stomach is so strong it could dissolve metal! But your stomach has a special mucus lining that protects it from getting hurt by the acid.

⏱️ The Digestive Journey

Food stays in your stomach for about 2 to 6 hours. During this time, it's mixed with acid and turned into a thick liquid called chyme.

After your stomach finishes its work, the chyme moves into your small intestine, where more digestion happens and your body absorbs nutrients!

🥗 Keeping Your Stomach Healthy

You can help keep your stomach healthy by:

  • Eating slowly and chewing food well
  • Eating healthy foods like fruits and vegetables
  • Drinking plenty of water
  • Not eating too much at once
  • Avoiding too many spicy or greasy foods

💡 Fun Fact

Your stomach growls when it's empty because it's pushing air and fluids around - it's your stomach saying "I'm hungry!"
