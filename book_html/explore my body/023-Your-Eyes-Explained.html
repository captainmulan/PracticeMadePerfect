📖 Your Eyes Explained

Learn more about your amazing eyes!

👁️ What Are Your Eyes?

Your eyes are special organs that let you see the world around you! They detect light and turn it into signals that your brain can understand.

Your eyes work like cameras - they let in light, focus it, and create an image that your brain can process!

💡 Fun Fact

Your eyes can distinguish about 10 million different colors - that's more than any computer screen can show!

🏗️ Parts of Your Eye

Your eye has many special parts that work together:

  • Cornea - Clear front part that protects your eye
  • Pupil - The dark center that lets light in
  • Iris - The colored part that controls pupil size
  • Lens - Focuses light onto your retina
  • Retina - Back of eye that detects light

🌈 How You See Color

Your retina has special cells called cones that detect different colors. You have three types of cones - one for red, one for green, and one for blue.

Your brain combines signals from these cones to create all the colors you see. That's why you can see millions of different colors!

💪 Keeping Your Eyes Healthy

You can help keep your eyes healthy by:

  • Wearing sunglasses to protect from UV rays
  • Taking breaks when reading or using screens
  • Eating foods with vitamin A like carrots
  • Not rubbing your eyes too hard
  • Getting regular eye check-ups

💡 Fun Fact

Your eyes blink about 15-20 times every minute to keep them moist and clean!
