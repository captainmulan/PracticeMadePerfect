📖 Your Lungs Explained

Learn more about your amazing lungs!

🫁 What Are Your Lungs?

Your lungs are two spongy organs in your chest that help you breathe! They take in oxygen from the air and remove carbon dioxide from your body.

Your lungs are pink and spongy when healthy, and they're protected by your rib cage like a shield!

💡 Fun Fact

Your lungs are the only organs that can float on water because they're filled with air!

💨 How Breathing Works

When you breathe in, a muscle called your diaphragm pulls down, creating space in your chest. Air rushes in through your nose or mouth and travels down your windpipe into your lungs.

Inside your lungs, tiny air sacs called alveoli take oxygen from the air and put it into your blood. At the same time, they take carbon dioxide from your blood so you can breathe it out!

🏗️ Parts of Your Lungs

Your lungs have special parts that help you breathe:

  • Trachea - Your windpipe that carries air to your lungs
  • Bronchi - Tubes that branch into your lungs
  • Alveoli - Tiny air sacs where oxygen enters your blood
  • Diaphragm - Muscle that helps you breathe in and out

💪 Keeping Your Lungs Healthy

You can help keep your lungs strong and healthy by:

  • Not smoking (when you're older)
  • Exercising and playing sports
  • Avoiding air pollution when possible
  • Staying away from secondhand smoke
  • Getting fresh air outdoors

💡 Fun Fact

Your right lung is bigger than your left lung - it has to make room for your heart!
