Little Programmer

Welcome! This small book teaches programming ideas for kids through short chapters and playful activities. Each chapter has simple explanations, examples, and a hands-on task you can try in the browser or with block tools like Scratch or Blockly.

  • Chapter 1 — What is programming

    Overview: Introduces programming as instructions and creative play. Activity: Give commands to make something happen.

  • Chapter 2 — What is a program

    Overview: Programs are step-by-step instructions (algorithms). Activity: Try the interactive "Move the Bunny" to see how a small program works.

    • Chapter 2.1 — Move the Bunny (interactive)
  • Chapter 3 — Events & Input

    Overview: Learn how programs react to clicks, key presses, and buttons. Activity: Make a sprite respond to a key or mouse.

    • Chapter 3.1 — Simple Event Program
  • Chapter 4 — Loops & Repetition

    Overview: Repeat actions with loops to make patterns, music, and movement. Activity: Make the bunny hop in a loop.

    • Chapter 4.1 — Looping Activity
  • Chapter 5 — Conditions & Decisions

    Overview: Use decisions (if/then) to make choices in programs. Activity: Make the bunny avoid obstacles.

    • Chapter 5.1 — Conditional Activity
  • Chapter 6 — Variables & Data

    Overview: Store numbers and words to keep score or remember things. Activity: Add a move counter or score to your game.

    • Chapter 6.1 — Using Variables
  • Chapter 7 — Debugging & Problem Solving

    Overview: How to find and fix mistakes. Activity: Read a broken program and fix it.

    • Chapter 7.1 — Debugging Puzzles
  • Chapter 8 — Projects & Sharing

    Overview: Combine lessons into a small project and share it. Activity: Create a final mini-game or story.

    • Chapter 8.1 — Final Project Ideas
  • Chapter 9 — Congratulations

    Overview: Celebrate your project and choose a fun next coding challenge.
