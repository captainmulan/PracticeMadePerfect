Chapter 1 — What is programming

Programming is giving instructions so computers can do things. For kids, it's like giving a recipe or a sequence of steps to make a toy move, draw a picture, or tell a story.

What you'll learn

  • What instructions are and why order matters (sequencing)
  • Simple examples of cause & effect
  • A short activity you can try right away

Try this

  1. Think of three steps to make a sandwich. Put them in order.
  2. Now swap the order — did it still work? That's sequencing.
  3. Try a tiny program: open the "Move the Bunny" activity in Chapter 2.1 and move the bunny one step at a time.
