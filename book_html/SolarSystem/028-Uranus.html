🔵 Uranus

The ice giant!

🌟 What is Uranus?

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is an ice giant. It's made mostly of water, ammonia, and methane ices, with a rocky core.

Uranus is the third-largest planet in our solar system. It's about 4 times wider than Earth!

🎨 Why is Uranus Blue?

Uranus is blue-green because of methane in its atmosphere. Methane absorbs red light and reflects blue light, making the planet look blue!

The methane also gives Uranus its beautiful, smooth appearance. Unlike Jupiter and Saturn, Uranus doesn't have many visible bands or storms.

💡 Fun Fact

Uranus is the coldest planetary atmosphere in the solar system!

🔄 The Rolling Planet

Uranus is very strange - it rolls on its side! Its axis is tilted at about 98 degrees, which means it rotates almost sideways compared to other planets.

Scientists think a huge space rock might have hit Uranus billions of years ago and knocked it over!

📏 How Big is Uranus?

Uranus is 50,724 km across - about 4 times wider than Earth. It's the third-largest planet, after Jupiter and Saturn.

🌙 Uranus's Moons

Uranus has 27 known moons! The moons are named after characters from plays by William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope.

The five largest moons are Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon. Titania is the biggest - about half the size of our Moon!

💍 Uranus's Rings

Uranus has rings too! They were discovered in 1977 and are much darker than Saturn's rings. They're made of dust and small rocks.

Uranus has 13 known rings. They're very thin and hard to see from Earth, but spacecraft have taken beautiful pictures of them!
