🪨 Mercury

The smallest planet!

🌟 What is Mercury?

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It's only slightly bigger than Earth's Moon!

Even though it's closest to the Sun, it's not the hottest planet (that's Venus!). Mercury has no atmosphere to trap heat.

🌡️ Hot and Cold

Mercury has extreme temperatures! During the day, it can get up to 430°C (800°F) - super hot! But at night, it drops to -180°C (-290°F) - freezing cold!

💡 Fun Fact

One day on Mercury is 59 Earth days long, but one year is only 88 Earth days!

📏 How Big is Mercury?

Mercury is tiny compared to other planets. It's only about 4,879 km across - that's about 1/3 the size of Earth!

🔭 What's on Mercury?

Mercury's surface is covered in craters, just like our Moon! It has lots of bumps and holes from space rocks hitting it long ago.

There are also big plains called "maria" (which means "seas" in Latin), but they're not real seas - they're just flat areas made of hardened lava!

🚀 Can We Visit Mercury?

Mercury is very hard to visit because it's so close to the Sun. The heat would melt most spacecraft! But scientists have sent robots to study it.

The MESSENGER spacecraft visited Mercury from 2011 to 2015, and the BepiColombo mission is there now!
