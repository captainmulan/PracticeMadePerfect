🔥 Venus

The hottest planet!

🌟 What is Venus?

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is Earth's "sister planet" because they're similar in size. But Venus is very different - it's the hottest planet in our solar system!

Even though Mercury is closer to the Sun, Venus is hotter because it has a thick atmosphere that traps heat like a blanket.

🌡️ Why is Venus So Hot?

Venus has a super thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide. This creates a "greenhouse effect" that traps heat from the Sun.

The surface temperature is about 465°C (870°F) - hot enough to melt lead! It's the same temperature everywhere on Venus, day or night.

💡 Fun Fact

Venus is hotter than Mercury even though Mercury is closer to the Sun!

📏 How Big is Venus?

Venus is almost the same size as Earth! It's about 12,104 km across - just 648 km smaller than Earth. That's why they're called sister planets.

🔄 Backwards Rotation

Venus is very strange - it rotates backwards! Most planets rotate counter-clockwise, but Venus rotates clockwise. This means the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east on Venus!

Also, a day on Venus (243 Earth days) is longer than its year (225 Earth days)!

☁️ Thick Clouds

Venus is covered in thick clouds made of sulfuric acid. These clouds are so thick that we can't see the surface from space!

The clouds also create very strong winds - up to 360 km/h (224 mph) at the cloud tops!

🏔️ Venus's Surface

Under the clouds, Venus has a rocky surface with volcanoes, mountains, and large plains. It has over 1,600 major volcanoes - more than any other planet!

The pressure on Venus's surface is 90 times stronger than Earth's - like being 1 km underwater!
