📖 Jupiter Explained

Learn more about the giant planet!

💨 Gas Giant

Jupiter is a gas giant, which means it doesn't have a solid surface like Earth. It's made mostly of hydrogen (about 90%) and helium (about 10%).

As you go deeper into Jupiter, the gas gets thicker and turns into a liquid. Scientists think there might be a solid core at the very center, but we're not sure!

💡 Fun Fact

Jupiter is almost entirely made of the same gases as the Sun!

🌀 The Great Red Spot

The Great Red Spot is a giant storm that's been raging for at least 400 years! It was first seen by astronomers in the 1600s.

The storm is about 1.3 times wider than Earth. Winds in the storm can reach 643 km/h (400 mph) - that's faster than any hurricane on Earth!

🌙 The Galilean Moons

Jupiter's four largest moons were discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610. They're called the Galilean moons:

  • Io - The most volcanically active place in the solar system
  • Europa - Has an icy shell with a possible ocean underneath
  • Ganymede - The largest moon in the solar system (bigger than Mercury!)
  • Callisto - The most heavily cratered object in the solar system

🔄 Fast Spinner

Jupiter spins very fast - a day is only about 10 hours long! This makes Jupiter bulge at the middle, like a squashed ball.

The equator is about 7,000 km wider than the poles! This fast spinning also creates strong jet streams and colorful bands in Jupiter's atmosphere.

🛡️ Jupiter as a Shield

Jupiter's strong gravity acts like a cosmic shield. It pulls in comets and asteroids that might otherwise hit the inner planets, including Earth.

Without Jupiter, Earth might get hit by dangerous space rocks much more often. Jupiter is like a big brother protecting us!

💡 Fun Fact

Jupiter has a faint ring system, but it's much smaller than Saturn's rings!

🔭 Exploring Jupiter

Many spacecraft have visited Jupiter:

  • Pioneer 10 & 11 - First spacecraft to fly by Jupiter (1973)
  • Voyager 1 & 2 - Took amazing photos of Jupiter (1979)
  • Galileo - First spacecraft to orbit Jupiter (1995-2003)
  • Jun - Currently studying Jupiter (arrived 2016)
