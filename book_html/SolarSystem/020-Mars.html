🌟 What is Mars?

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish color. The color comes from iron oxide (rust) in the soil!

Mars is smaller than Earth and has a thinner atmosphere. It's a cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide.

🌡️ Cold and Dry

Mars is very cold! The average temperature is about -60°C (-80°F). It can get as cold as -125°C (-195°F) at the poles and as warm as 20°C (68°F) at the equator.

Mars is also very dry. It has polar ice caps made of water ice and dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide), but liquid water doesn't exist on the surface today.

💡 Fun Fact

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system - Olympus Mons!

📏 How Big is Mars?

Mars is about half the size of Earth - 6,779 km across. It has about 38% of Earth's gravity, so you could jump much higher there!

🏔️ Mars's Surface

Mars has amazing surface features:

  • Olympus Mons - The largest volcano (3x taller than Mount Everest!)
  • Valles Marineris - A huge canyon system (4,000 km long!)
  • Polar Ice Caps - Made of water ice and dry ice
  • Dust storms - Can cover the whole planet!

🤖 Exploring Mars

Many robots have visited Mars! Scientists have sent:

  • Rovers - Like Curiosity and Perseverance, which drive around
  • Landers - Like InSight, which stay in one place
  • Orbiters - Spacecraft that circle Mars
  • Helicopters - Like Ingenuity, the first aircraft on another planet!

🌙 Mars's Moons

Mars has two small moons named Phobos and Deimos. They're shaped like potatoes and are probably captured asteroids!

Phobos is getting closer to Mars and might crash into it in about 50 million years!
