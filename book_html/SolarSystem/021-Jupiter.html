🟠 Jupiter

The giant planet!

🌟 What is Jupiter?

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system! It's a gas giant, which means it's made mostly of gas and doesn't have a solid surface like Earth.

Jupiter is so big that over 1,300 Earths could fit inside it! It's also very heavy - it's 2.5 times heavier than all the other planets combined!

🌀 The Great Red Spot

Jupiter has a famous storm called the Great Red Spot. It's a giant storm that's been going for hundreds of years!

The storm is so big that 2-3 Earths could fit inside it! Scientists aren't sure why it's red or how long it will last.

💡 Fun Fact

The Great Red Spot is shrinking - it used to be much bigger!

📏 How Big is Jupiter?

Jupiter is enormous - 139,820 km across! That's about 11 times wider than Earth. If Jupiter were a basketball, Earth would be the size of a grape!

🌙 Jupiter's Moons

Jupiter has at least 95 moons! The four biggest ones are called the Galilean moons:

  • Io - Has active volcanoes
  • Europa - Might have an ocean under ice
  • Ganymede - The biggest moon in the solar system
  • Callisto - Has many craters

💨 Fast Rotation

Jupiter spins very fast! A day on Jupiter is only about 10 hours long. This fast spinning makes Jupiter bulge at the middle - it's not a perfect sphere!

The fast rotation also creates strong winds and storms on Jupiter.

🛡️ Jupiter's Protection

Jupiter acts like a cosmic vacuum cleaner! Its strong gravity pulls in space rocks and comets that might otherwise hit Earth.

In 1994, a comet called Shoemaker-Levy 9 crashed into Jupiter. If it had hit Earth instead, it would have caused huge damage!
