🌟 What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is so strong that nothing — not even light — can escape once it gets too close!

Black holes form when very massive stars collapse at the end of their lives. They are invisible, but we can detect them by watching nearby stars and gas.

🌀 The Event Horizon

The event horizon is the "point of no return" around a black hole. Cross it and you cannot get back out — gravity wins!

💡 Fun Fact

Time slows down near a black hole! If you could watch from far away, things near the hole would look like they move in slow motion.

🔭 How We Find Them

Scientists spot black holes by watching stars orbit something invisible, or by seeing hot gas swirl and glow before it falls in.

In 2019, astronomers took the first picture of a black hole's shadow — a huge achievement!
