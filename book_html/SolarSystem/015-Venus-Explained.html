📚 Venus Explained

Learn more about the hottest planet!

🌡️ The Greenhouse Effect

Venus's thick atmosphere creates a powerful greenhouse effect. Carbon dioxide traps heat from the Sun, just like glass traps heat in a greenhouse.

This effect is so strong that Venus is hotter than Mercury, even though Mercury is closer to the Sun. The heat can't escape into space!

💡 Fun Fact

Venus's greenhouse effect is 90 times stronger than Earth's!

🔄 Backwards Day

Venus is the only planet that rotates backwards (retrograde rotation). This means if you could stand on Venus, you would see the Sun rise in the west and set in the east!

Scientists aren't sure why Venus rotates this way. It might have been hit by a huge space rock long ago that knocked it backwards.

☁️ The Clouds of Venus

Venus is covered in thick yellow clouds made of sulfuric acid. These clouds are about 20-30 km thick and reflect a lot of sunlight, making Venus very bright in our sky.

The clouds also create super strong winds - up to 360 km/h (224 mph) at the cloud tops! But on the surface, the air barely moves.

🏔️ Volcanoes Everywhere

Venus has more volcanoes than any other planet - over 1,600 major ones! Some of these might still be active today.

The surface also has large plains, high mountains, and deep valleys. The highest mountain on Venus is Maxwell Montes, which is 11 km high!

💡 Fun Fact

Venus's surface is about 500 million years old - younger than most planets!

🔭 Exploring Venus

Many spacecraft have visited Venus:

  • Venera - Soviet spacecraft that landed on Venus in the 1970s
  • Magellan - Mapped Venus's surface with radar in the 1990s
  • Venus Express - European mission from 2006-2014
  • Akatsuki - Japanese mission that arrived in 2015

🌍 Earth's Sister Planet

Venus and Earth are similar in size and are sometimes called "sister planets." But they're very different!

Venus has no water, no life, and a toxic atmosphere. It shows us what Earth might be like if the greenhouse effect got out of control.
