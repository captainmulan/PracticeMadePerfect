🌟 What is a Meteor?

A meteor is a streak of light you see when a space rock burns up in Earth's atmosphere. Kids often call them shooting stars!

Before it enters the air, the rock is called a meteoroid. If a piece survives and lands on the ground, it becomes a meteorite.

🔥 Burning Bright

Meteors glow because friction heats them up. They can travel up to 70 km per second — that's super fast!

💡 Fun Fact

Most meteors are tiny — about the size of a grain of sand!

🌠 Meteor Showers

Sometimes Earth passes through a trail of dust left by a comet. Then we see many meteors in one night — a meteor shower!

Famous showers include the Perseids in August and the Geminids in December. Grab a blanket and look up!
