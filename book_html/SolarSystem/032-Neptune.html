🌟 What is Neptune?

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It's an ice giant like Uranus, made mostly of water, ammonia, and methane ices.

Neptune is the fourth-largest planet and is very similar to Uranus in size and composition. It's the first planet discovered through mathematical prediction!

💨 The Windiest Planet

Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system! Winds can reach up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph) - that's faster than the speed of sound on Earth!

These super-fast winds create huge storms in Neptune's atmosphere. The planet also has a Great Dark Spot, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

💡 Fun Fact

Neptune's winds are so fast they could blow away a house in seconds!

📏 How Big is Neptune?

Neptune is 49,244 km across - about 4 times wider than Earth. It's almost the same size as Uranus!

🌙 Neptune's Moons

Neptune has 16 known moons. The largest one is Triton, which is bigger than Pluto!

Triton is very special - it orbits Neptune backwards (retrograde orbit). Scientists think Triton might have been a dwarf planet that Neptune captured long ago!

🔭 Discovery of Neptune

Neptune was the first planet discovered through mathematics! Astronomers noticed that Uranus wasn't moving the way it should, and calculated that another planet must be pulling on it.

In 1846, astronomers looked where the math said Neptune should be - and there it was! This was a huge triumph for science!

❄️ Cold and Dark

Neptune is very far from the Sun, so it's very cold and dark. The average temperature is about -214°C (-353°F).

Even though it's so far away, sunlight still reaches Neptune - it's just about 900 times dimmer than sunlight on Earth!
