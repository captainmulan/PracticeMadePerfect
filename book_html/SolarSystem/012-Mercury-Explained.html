📚 Mercury Explained

Learn more about the smallest planet!

🌡️ Why is Mercury So Hot and Cold?

Mercury has no atmosphere - that means there's no air to trap heat like on Earth. During the day, the Sun heats up the surface quickly. At night, all that heat escapes into space because there's nothing to hold it in!

This is why Mercury has such extreme temperature changes between day and night.

💡 Fun Fact

Mercury's temperature can change by 600°C in a single day!

🔄 Strange Days and Years

Mercury has a very strange day and year cycle. One day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) takes 59 Earth days. But one year (orbit around the Sun) only takes 88 Earth days!

This means Mercury's day is longer than its year! If you lived on Mercury, you'd have birthdays more often than you'd have bedtimes!

🕳️ Mercury's Surface

Mercury looks a lot like our Moon. It's covered in craters from space rocks hitting it billions of years ago. The biggest crater is called Caloris Basin - it's huge!

There are also smooth plains called "maria" (seas in Latin), but they're made of hardened lava, not water. Mercury has no liquid water at all!

❄️ Ice on Mercury?

Even though Mercury is super hot, scientists have found ice in some craters near the poles! These craters are always in shadow, so the Sun never shines there.

The ice has been there for billions of years, protected from the Sun's heat!

💡 Fun Fact

Mercury might have more ice than our Moon!

🔭 Exploring Mercury

Scientists have sent spacecraft to study Mercury:

  • MARINER 10 - First spacecraft to visit Mercury in 1974
  • MESSENGER - Orbited Mercury from 2011 to 2015
  • BepiColombo - Arrived in 2025, still studying it

These missions help us learn more about this mysterious little planet!

🏋️ Gravity on Mercury

Mercury is much smaller than Earth, so its gravity is weaker too. If you weigh 30 kg on Earth, you would only weigh about 11 kg on Mercury!

You could jump much higher and lift much heavier things on Mercury!
