💍 Saturn

The ringed planet!

🌟 What is Saturn?

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is famous for its beautiful rings! It's a gas giant like Jupiter, made mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Saturn is the second-largest planet in our solar system. It's so big that over 750 Earths could fit inside it!

💍 Saturn's Amazing Rings

Saturn's rings are made of billions of pieces of ice and rock! The pieces range from tiny grains to house-sized chunks.

The rings are very wide - about 282,000 km across - but very thin, only about 10 meters thick in some places!

💡 Fun Fact

Saturn's rings are mostly made of water ice - like frozen snowflakes!

📏 How Big is Saturn?

Saturn is huge - 116,460 km across! It's about 9 times wider than Earth. Like Jupiter, Saturn spins very fast, which makes it bulge at the middle.

🌙 Saturn's Moons

Saturn has at least 146 moons - more than any other planet! The biggest one is Titan, which is larger than Mercury!

Titan is special because it has a thick atmosphere and liquid lakes on its surface - but the lakes are made of methane, not water!

🎈 Could You Float on Saturn?

Saturn is the only planet that would float in water! This is because it's less dense than water. If you had a big enough bathtub, Saturn would float!

This is because Saturn is made mostly of gas, which is very light compared to water or rock.

🌀 Hexagonal Storm

Saturn has a strange hexagonal (six-sided) storm at its north pole! This storm has been there for decades and is about 30,000 km across.

Scientists aren't sure why the storm is hexagonal - it's a mystery they're still studying!
