📚 The Sun Explained

Learn more about our amazing star!

⚛️ What is the Sun Made Of?

The Sun is made mostly of two gases: hydrogen (about 73%) and helium (about 25%). The remaining 2% is made of other elements like oxygen, carbon, and iron.

The Sun creates energy by turning hydrogen into helium in a process called nuclear fusion. This happens in the Sun's core, where it's super hot!

💡 Fun Fact

The Sun uses 600 million tons of hydrogen every second!

🔥 Parts of the Sun

The Sun has different layers, just like Earth has layers:

  • Core - The hottest part where energy is made
  • Radiative Zone - Energy moves through this layer
  • Convective Zone - Hot gas rises and cools gas falls
  • Photosphere - The visible surface we see
  • Corona - The outer atmosphere, super hot!

🌡️ Solar Activity

The Sun is very active! It has things called:

  • Sunspots - Darker, cooler areas on the surface
  • Solar Flares - Big bursts of energy and light
  • Coronal Mass Ejections - Huge clouds of gas that fly into space

Sometimes these can affect Earth! They might cause beautiful auroras (northern lights) or even mess with satellites and power grids.

🌅 Day and Night

Earth rotates (spins) on its axis once every 24 hours. This is why we have day and night. When part of Earth faces the Sun, it's daytime. When it faces away, it's nighttime.

The Sun always rises in the east and sets in the west because of Earth's rotation!

💡 Fun Fact

The Sun is actually a star, just like the stars you see at night - it's just much closer to us!

🔭 The Sun's Future

The Sun has been shining for about 4.6 billion years and will continue for about 5 billion more years. Eventually, it will run out of hydrogen and become a red giant, then a white dwarf.

But don't worry - that won't happen for a very, very long time!

🛡️ Staying Safe

The Sun gives us life, but we need to be careful too:

  • Never look directly at the Sun - it can hurt your eyes!
  • Wear sunscreen to protect your skin from UV rays
  • Wear sunglasses on bright days
  • Stay in the shade during the hottest part of the day
